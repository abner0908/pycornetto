235          Compute the semantic similarity as decribed in Philip Resnik's paper 
236          "Using Information Content to Evaluate Semantic Similarity in a 
237          Taxonomy" (1995). It is defined for a pair of concepts c1 and c2 as: 
238           
239          argmax [IC(c1) + IC(c2) - 2 * IC(lcs)] for all lcs in LCS(c1, c2) 
240           
241          In other words, the maximum value of the information content over all  
242          least common subsumers of two concepts. An important point is that the  
243          counts of an LCS, as used in computing its probabilty, is the sum of  
244          its own count plus the counts of all concepts that it subsumes. 
245           
246          As suggested by Resnik, it can be extended to _word_ similarity by 
247          taking the maximum over the scores for all concepts that are senses of 
248          the word. This means that if just two words are specified - without a 
249          category or sense - two sets of matching lexical units are retrieved. 
250          For every combination of lexical units from these two sets, the LCS is 
251          computed (if any), and the one with the maximum information content is 
252          selected. 
253           
254          If no LCS is found, this can mean two things:         
255              1. The two words have no LCS because they truely have nothing  
256                 in common. In this case we assume the LCS is zero and therefore  
257                 we return zero. 
258              2. The two words should have something in common, but the correct  
259                 LCS is not present in the Cornetto database. However, since  
260                 there is no way to know this, we consider this the same as (1), 
261                 and zero is returned. 
262           
263          There are two more marginal cases: 
264              1. No lexical units in the Cornetto database match the  
265                 specifications.  
266              2. All LCS have a subcount of zero, and no smoothing was applied, 
267                 so its IC is undefined.  
268          In both cases None is returned. 
269           
270          Notice that it is difficult to compare Resnik's word similarities, 
271          because they depend on the subcounts. With identical words, for instance, 
272          resnik_sim("iets", "iets") = 1.3113543459343666 whereas 
273          resnik_sim("spotje", "spotje") = 25.141834494846584

284

289          @rtype: float or None

292

294          if self.get_lex_units(lu_spec1) and self.get_lex_units(lu_spec2): 
295              lcs_ics = [ self._IC(lcs, True, smooth, cat_totals) 
296                          for lcs in self.least_common_subsumers(lu_spec1,  
297                                                                 lu_spec2, 
298                                                                 format="raw") ]
