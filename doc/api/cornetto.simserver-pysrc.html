209

211              LU_SPEC_2 string: second lexical unit specification 
212              SMOOTH bool: smooth counts by adding one to lexical units

215                  instead of overall total count

217               
218              SIM float or False: similarity score greater than or equal  
219                  to zero, or False 
220                   
221          Remarks: 
222           
223              Resnik's similarity is defined for a pair of concepts c1 and c2 
224              as: 
225               
226                  argmax [IC(c1) + IC(c2) - 2 * IC(lcs)] for all lcs in LCS(c1, c2) 
227               
228              In other words, the maximum value of the information content over 
229              all least common subsumers of two concepts. An important point is 
230              that the counts of an LCS, as used in computing its probabilty, is 
231              the sum of its own count plus the counts of all concepts that it 
232              subsumes. 
233               
234              As suggested by Resnik, it can be extended to _word_ similarity by 
235              taking the maximum over the scores for all concepts that are 
236              senses of the word. This means that if just two words are 
237              specified - without a category or sense - two sets of matching 
238              lexical units are retrieved. For every combination of lexical 
239              units from these two sets, the LCS is computed (if any), and the 
240              one with the maximum information content is selected. 
241               
242              If no LCS is found, this can mean two things:  
243               
244                  1. The two words have no LCS because they truely have  
245                     nothing in common. In this case we assume the LCS is zero and  
246                     therefore we return zero. 
247                  2. The two words should have something in common, but the  
248                     correct LCS is not present in the Cornetto database.  
249                     However, since there is no way to know this, we consider this  
250                     the same as (1), and zero is returned. 
251               
252              There are two more marginal cases: 
253               
254                  1. No lexical units in the Cornetto database match the  
255                     specifications.  
256                  2. All LCS have a subcount of zero, and no smoothing was applied, 
257                     so its IC is undefined.  
258                      
259              In both cases False is returned. 
260               
261              Notice that it is difficult to compare Resnik's word similarities, 
262              because they depend on the subcounts. With identical words, for 
263              instance: 
264               
265                  resnik_sim("iets", "iets") = 1.3113543459343666 whereas 
266                  resnik_sim("spotje", "spotje") = 25.141834494846584 
267          """
